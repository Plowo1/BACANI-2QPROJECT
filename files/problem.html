What is the problem?

Overpopulation increases pressure on resources and services, particularly in developing regions. Overcrowding, limited infrastructure, and food insecurity become daily challenges for millions.

Causes & contributing factors

  • Insufficient access to family planning
  • Low levels of education for women
  • Poverty and lack of social safety nets
  • Migration to cities without infrastructure growth

Effects on communities & environment

Resource depletion, greater waste generation, and loss of biodiversity are direct consequences. These effects worsen poverty and inequality when not properly addressed.

Case: Developing regions

Rapid population growth strains water, sanitation, and housing systems — leading to public health risks and environmental degradation.
